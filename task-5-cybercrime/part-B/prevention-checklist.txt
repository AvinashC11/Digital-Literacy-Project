PURPOSE:
This checklist provides specific, actionable steps for college students in
India to protect themselves from cybercrime — including phishing, UPI fraud,
identity theft, cyberbullying, and ransomware.

================================================================================
              🔐  STAY SAFE ONLINE — PREVENTION CHECKLIST
================================================================================

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 SECTION 1 — ACCOUNT & PASSWORD SECURITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 ☐  TIP 1 — Use Strong, Unique Passwords for Every Account
    Never use the same password across multiple platforms. Create passwords
    that are at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase
    letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters (e.g.
    R@hul#2024Vit!). Use a trusted password manager such as Bitwarden or
    Google Password Manager to store them securely.

 ☐  TIP 2 — Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on All Accounts
    Always activate 2FA on your email, social media, and academic portals.
    This adds a second layer of security — even if someone obtains your
    password, they cannot access your account without the second verification
    step (OTP or authentication app). Go to Settings → Security → Two-Factor
    Authentication on each platform to enable it.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 SECTION 2 — 💳 FINANCIAL & UPI SAFETY  [MANDATORY — UPI SPECIFIC]
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 ☐  TIP 3 — Never Share Your UPI PIN, OTP, or Bank Details with Anyone
    ⚠️  UPI SAFETY — CRITICAL
    Your UPI PIN is equivalent to your ATM PIN — it must never be shared
    with anyone, including people claiming to be bank officials, customer
    support agents, or friends. Legitimate banks and UPI apps (GPay, PhonePe,
    Paytm) will NEVER ask for your PIN or OTP over a call, message, or email.
    If someone asks for it, it is a scam. Hang up immediately.

 ☐  TIP 4 — Always Verify Before Accepting or Sending UPI Payments
    ⚠️  UPI SAFETY — CRITICAL
    Be aware that scammers send COLLECT REQUESTS — which require YOU to enter
    your PIN to receive money. This is a common fraud: you enter your PIN
    thinking you are receiving money, but you are actually sending it. Rule:
    You NEVER need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If a collect
    request appears unexpectedly, decline it immediately and block the sender.
    Always double-check the recipient's name and UPI ID before confirming any
    payment.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 SECTION 3 — PHISHING & ONLINE FRAUD AWARENESS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 ☐  TIP 5 — Do Not Click on Suspicious Links in Emails, SMS, or DMs
    Phishing is one of the most common cybercrimes targeting students.
    Attackers send fake links disguised as college portals, scholarship forms,
    job offers, or prize notifications. Always verify the sender's email
    address carefully (e.g. vitbhopal.ac.in vs vitbhopa1.ac.in — note the
    number "1" replacing the letter "l"). When in doubt, go directly to the
    official website by typing the URL in your browser rather than clicking
    any link.

 ☐  TIP 6 — Verify the Authenticity of Websites Before Entering Any Data
    Before entering your login credentials, personal details, or payment
    information on any website, check that the URL begins with "https://"
    and that there is a padlock icon in the browser address bar. Avoid
    entering sensitive information on websites that display security warnings
    or have suspicious domain names.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 SECTION 4 — DEVICE & NETWORK SAFETY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 ☐  TIP 7 — Avoid Using Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Activities
    Public Wi-Fi networks in cafes, malls, bus stands, and railway stations
    are frequently unsecured and can be monitored by attackers. Never access
    your college portal, internet banking, UPI apps, or email accounts on
    public Wi-Fi. If necessary, use your mobile data or a trusted VPN service
    for any sensitive activity.

 ☐  TIP 8 — Keep Your Devices, Apps, and Antivirus Software Updated
    Cybercriminals exploit known vulnerabilities in outdated software to
    install malware and ransomware on devices. Always keep your phone and
    laptop operating systems, applications, and antivirus software up to date.
    Enable automatic updates wherever possible. Do not download apps or
    software from unofficial or unverified sources.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
 SECTION 5 — SOCIAL MEDIA & DIGITAL IDENTITY PROTECTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 ☐  TIP 9 — Protect Your Personal Information on Social Media
    Do not publicly display your phone number, home address, date of birth,
    or Aadhaar/PAN details on any social media platform. Cybercriminals use
    this information for identity theft — fraudulently applying for loans,
    SIM swaps, or government schemes in your name. Review your privacy
    settings on Instagram, Facebook, and Snapchat regularly.

 ☐  TIP 10 — Be Cautious of Fake Job, Scholarship, and Lottery Offers
    College students are frequently targeted with fake internship offers,
    scholarship notifications, and prize claims via WhatsApp, Telegram, and
    email. These messages typically ask you to pay a "registration fee" or
    "processing charge" to claim the offer. Legitimate scholarships and
    internships never require upfront payments. Always verify through the
    official company or institution website before responding.

                    🚨  REPORTING CHANNELS — INDIA
================================================================================

  If you or someone you know has been a victim of cybercrime, report
  immediately through the following official channels:

  🌐  National Cyber Crime Portal  :  https://cybercrime.gov.in
      (File a complaint online — available 24x7)

  📞  Cyber Crime Helpline         :  1930
      (Call to report financial cyber fraud — available 24x7)

  📋  Legal Reference              :  IT Act, 2000 & IT (Amendment) Act, 2008
      (Governs all cybercrimes in India — penalties include imprisonment
       and fines for offences such as hacking, fraud, and identity theft)

  ⚠️  IMPORTANT: In case of UPI or online financial fraud, report to
      Helpline 1930 IMMEDIATELY — the sooner you report, the higher the
      chance of recovering your money before the transaction is settled.
