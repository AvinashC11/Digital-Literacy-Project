PURPOSE:
This checklist serves as a practical guide for college students to maintain
a responsible, professional, and safe presence across social media platforms.

================================================================================
                          ✅  DO'S
================================================================================

 ✅ DO 1 — Keep Your Profile Professional
    Maintain a clean, professional profile across all platforms. Use a clear
    profile photo, a real name, and an appropriate bio. Recruiters and faculty
    often review student profiles — first impressions matter.

 ✅ DO 2 — Think Before You Post
    Always pause and ask yourself: "Would I be comfortable if my professor,
    parent, or future employer saw this?" If the answer is no, do not post it.
    Once something is online, it is nearly impossible to fully erase.

 ✅ DO 3 — Use Privacy Settings Wisely
    Regularly review and update your privacy settings on all platforms
    (Instagram, Facebook, Snapchat, etc.). Limit personal information — such
    as your location, phone number, and daily routine — to trusted connections
    only.

 ✅ DO 4 — Engage Respectfully and Constructively
    When participating in discussions, debates, or comment sections, keep your
    language respectful and your arguments factual. Constructive engagement
    builds your reputation as a thoughtful and mature individual.

 ✅ DO 5 — Verify Information Before Sharing
    Always check the credibility of news, images, or claims before sharing
    them. Spreading misinformation — even unintentionally — can damage your
    credibility and contribute to public harm.

 ✅ DO 6 — Use Social Media for Learning and Networking
    Follow industry leaders, academic pages, and professional communities
    relevant to your field. Platforms like LinkedIn and Twitter/X can be
    powerful tools for career growth when used intentionally.

 ✅ DO 7 — Report Harmful or Abusive Content
    If you encounter cyberbullying, harassment, hate speech, or illegal
    content, use the platform's reporting tools. You can also report
    cybercrimes at cybercrime.gov.in or call Helpline 1930.

================================================================================
                          ❌  DON'TS
================================================================================

 ❌ DON'T 1 — Do Not Share Personal or Sensitive Information Publicly
    Never post your home address, phone number, Aadhaar details, bank
    information, or daily schedule on any public platform. This information
    can be exploited for identity theft, fraud, or physical harm.

 ❌ DON'T 2 — Do Not Engage in or Tolerate Cyberbullying
    Never post, share, or like content that mocks, harasses, or demeans
    another individual. Cyberbullying has serious psychological consequences
    for victims and legal consequences for perpetrators under the IT Act, 2000.

 ❌ DON'T 3 — Do Not Post Impulsively During Emotional Moments
    Avoid posting when you are angry, upset, or under the influence. Emotional
    posts are often regretted and can permanently affect your academic and
    professional reputation.

 ❌ DON'T 4 — Do Not Accept Requests from Unknown Individuals
    Do not connect with, follow back, or engage privately with strangers on
    any platform. Fake profiles are commonly used for scams, phishing, and
    social engineering attacks targeting college students.

 ❌ DON'T 5 — Do Not Share Exam Papers, Internal Marks, or College Notices
    Circulating confidential academic content — such as question papers,
    internal assessment marks, or official notices — without authorisation
    is a violation of institutional policy and can lead to disciplinary action.

 ❌ DON'T 6 — Do Not Click on Suspicious Links Shared via DMs or Posts
    Phishing links are frequently distributed through social media direct
    messages, even from accounts that appear to belong to friends. Always
    verify the source before clicking any link.

 ❌ DON'T 7 — Do Not Use Social Media During Academic Hours Irresponsibly
    Excessive and unregulated social media use during lectures, labs, or
    study time directly impacts academic performance. Set boundaries and use
    screen-time management tools if needed.
